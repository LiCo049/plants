Common Name: Dutch iris

Botanical Name: Iris × hollandica

Chinese Name: 荷兰鸢尾

Cultivation
Grow in well-drained, fertile, neutral to slightly acid soil in full sun.

The Dutch Iris' unique veining and flashes of yellows make it a plant full of character, and can make a real impact when planted indrifts through the border. The Dutch Iris makes an exquisite cut flower, and with their strong, straight stems, you won't be able to resist placing them in your summer cut flower displays.The Dutch Iris belongs among the most hardy and accommodating plants, flourishing everywhere in the country in almost any condition. It's no surprise then, that the Dutch Iris has established itself as a garden favourite. 7-8cm bulbs supplied. Flowers June to July. Height 55cm.

Illuminate your late-spring garden with a rainbow of glamorous Dutch Irises, the ideal hardy plant for growing in borders, containers and for use as cut flowers! The alluring Dutch Iris 'Mixed Colours' flowers with amazingly elaborate blooms in a stunning blend of two-tone, single coloured and trendy copper-hued varieties, so you'll be able to create easy and colourful groups all around the garden.

Dutch Irises are also strong naturalisers, meaning that from late spring through to early summer each year you'll get the pleasure of a beautifully varied display from this colourful mix. Plus, establishing into larger clumps each spring, you'll not have good strong chunks of colour in the garden, but plenty of excellent-quality long-stemmed cutting material for use in indoor arrangements too!
