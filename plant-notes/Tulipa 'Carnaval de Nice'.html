Common Name: Double late tulipa 'Carnaval de Nice'

Botanical Name: Tulipa 'Carnaval de Nice'

Chinese Name: 郁金香

'Carnaval de Nice' is a double-flowered tulip to 40cm in height, with grey-green leaves narrowly edged with white, the flowers white, flamed and feathered with deep red

You’ll be hard pushed to find prettier, more flamboyant tulips. The fully double, creamy white blooms boast raspberry red streaks, that give a fabulous candy cane effect. The varigated foliage is equally attractive - each leaf edged with an ivory margin. This carnival of colour appears a little later than other tulips, closing the gap between spring and summer colour in your garden. Its easy to see why Tulip 'Carnaval de Nice' holds a prestigious RHS AGM. Perfect for bringing colour and personality to your beds, borders and patio pots. Height: 45cm (18"). Spread: 15cm (6").

Culinary note: Some parts of these flowers are edible. However,some people have had strong allergic reactions to Tulip flowers. If touching them causes a rash, numbness etc. then Tulip flowers should not be eaten.

Plant tulip bulbs in late summer or autumn. Tulips will grow well in any moist, well drained soil, except for particularly wet soils. Choose a sunny position that is sheltered from strong winds. When planting tulip bulbs avoid shallow planting as this may reduce the winter cold period that is essential for tulip bulbs to produce flowers in spring. Plant them at a depth of 15cm (6") and at a distance of 13cm (5") apart.
