Common Name: tulip 'La Belle Époque'

Botanical Name: Tulipa 'La Belle Époque'

Chinese Name: 郁金香




Other references
RHS
mr-fothergills
hayloft

'La Belle Époque' bears flowers in shades of apricot and pale bronze with black anthers. Reaches a height of around 45cm while flowering in late spring

Double Early Group tulips flower in mid-spring, with double, bowl-shaped flowers, sometimes flecked with a contrasting colour

In French 'La Belle Epoque' translates to 'A beautiful era' and Tulip La Belle Epoque really does take you back to sepia toned memories of gardens or still life oil paintings. The trend for latte shades is still strong for our blooms and this variety certainly fits the bill. Vintage and as chic as can be Tulip La Belle Epoque stands out in its beauty with dusky peach colouration with peony type flowers making them truly stunning for any cut flower arrangement. Stunning when punctuating any spring border giving an air of romanticism before the roses come into flower. Perfectly paired with creamy toned tulips such as 'Silk Road' or tulip 'Mondial' or pink and purple types to create a timeless and elegant planting scheme. Plant in the late Autumn and Tulip La Belle Epoque will reward you with a long flowering season to really make the most of this charming cultivar.
