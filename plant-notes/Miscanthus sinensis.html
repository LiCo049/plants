Often known as Chinese Silver Grass, Miscanthus sinensis 'Morning Light' Grass has a tufted, fountainous growing habit with slender, silvery-green leaves. Amongst the slender, deciduous leaves of Miscanthus sinensis 'Morning Light', small pink tinged panicles of flowers can be found in Autumn.

Miscanthus are reliable grasses that provide interest over a long period. Fountains of foliage grow up in early summer and bear plumes of white or pink flowers in late summer. As winter approaches these bleach into shades of biscuity brown. Tall cultivars suit the back of borders and smaller cultivars mingle well in beds with plants with bold, colourful flowers.

Miscanthus sinensis ‘Malepartus’ is an attractive ornamental grass with fine, arching leaves with a distinctive central white stripe, which take on beautiful orange and red hues in autumn. In summer it bears spectacular red-brown, silky feathery blooms, which appear to glow in the evening light. Quick to establish, it’s perfect for a mixed ornamental border or gravel garden.

Grow Miscanthus sinensis ‘Malepartus’ in moist but well-drained soil in full sun. Cut back to the ground in late-winter to early spring.
