Common Name: camass Caerulea Group, Indian Hyacinth

Botanical Name: Camassia leichtlinii subsp. suksdorfii Caerulea Group

Chinese Name: 北美百合，印度风信子

The whopping azure-blue camassia which naturalises so successfully in garden or grass. Here they are in the beautiful walled garden at Parham in West Sussex, where they transform a rough lawn.

Bulbs will form clumps and gradually spread so plant somewhere it can naturalise happily. Mulch well in autumn, as this will need good frost protection.

Preferably plant in moist but well-drained fertile, humus-rich soil in sun or part shade, providing a winter mulch. Plant bulbs 10cm deep in autumn.

Tall spires of star-shaped, mid-blue to lilac flowers appear above strap-like leaves in late spring. Originally from damp meadowlands in North America, this bulbous perennial is perfect for naturalising in a wildflower meadow. It will happily grow in moist soil in sun or partial shade as long as the soil doesn't become waterlogged. Take advantage of its brief flowering season to brighten up a border before summer perennials take over.
